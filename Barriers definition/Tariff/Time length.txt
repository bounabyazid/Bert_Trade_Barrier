Length: tariff surcharge versus countervailing duty
Previously mentioned protective and discriminatory tariffs, they are similar in some aspects, might be further classified according to the length of time it lasts. A tariff surcharge is a temporary action, while countervailing duty is a permanent surcharge, which is imposed on certain imports of products subsidized by exporting government. The aim of those duties is to offset a discount or special advantage allowed by the foreign government. Export subsidies are most commonly met in form of rebating certain taxes in case of exporting goods.
